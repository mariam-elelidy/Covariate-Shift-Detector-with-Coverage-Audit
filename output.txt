── WHY q STAYS CONSTANT: THE CORE FAILURE MECHANISM ────────────────────────
  q = 1.1953 across all 14 shift levels.
  This is not a bug — it is exactly how conformal prediction is designed.
  q is calibrated on the calibration set (in-distribution) and applied to
  test data. The model has no mechanism to detect that test data has drifted.

  Under covariate shift:
  - Test residuals = |y_test - ŷ_test|
  - = |X_te@w_true + 0.4*(X_te[:,0]+delta)² + noise - X_te@w_pred|
  - The (X_te[:,0]+delta)² term grows with delta²
  - Test residuals grow; q is too small to cover them
  - Coverage falls, but the model continues to report 90% confidence

  This is a silent failure. The model doesn't know it's failing. The intervals
  look exactly the same (width = 2*q = 2.391) whether delta=0 or delta=4.
  Only an external shift detector can flag it.

CROSS-OBSERVATION NOTES
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. Spearman rho = -0.986 (empirical, not assumed).
   The MMD-coverage relationship is measured, not derived from theory.
   This is the artifact's primary claim: MMD is a reliable proxy for
   coverage degradation under covariate shift, and this can be quantified.

2. The alarm fires BEFORE the guarantee fails.
   At delta=0.4: alarm=YES, coverage=0.940 (still above target).
   At delta=1.0: coverage=0.840 (below target, guarantee void).
   Early detection window: delta 0.4 to 1.0 — the system warns while
   predictions are still reliable.

3. MMD saturates; coverage does not.
   MMD² reaches ~0.0665 at delta=2.5 and stays there.
   Coverage continues to drop from 0.28 → 0.027 (delta 2.5 → 4.0).
   At large shifts, MMD is no longer informative about HOW MUCH coverage
   has degraded — only that a severe shift exists.
   This is a limitation: MMD is sensitive to distributional differences
   but not monotonically tied to coverage at all shift magnitudes.

4. Bias grows linearly with delta; q is constant.
   Bias at delta=0: +0.070 (small, near-symmetric)
   Bias at delta=4: -6.178 (the model systematically over-predicts by 6 units)
   q = 1.195 throughout. The intervals are ±1.195 around a prediction that
   is wrong by 6. This is not a calibration failure in the traditional sense —
   it is a model validity failure that calibration cannot detect or fix.

5. Sigma selection matters.
   Using sigma = 0.3 * median heuristic (sigma=0.98) produces clean detection.
   The full median (sigma=3.27) saturates too quickly — small shifts produce
   MMD values indistinguishable from the null. Documenting sigma as a
   hyperparameter of the detection system (not a free parameter) is essential.
